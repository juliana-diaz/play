Advance DEI

An invitation to advance DEI in your work

Advance DEI is an opportunity for BMGF grantees to embark on a two-and-a-half year journey to infuse more diversity, equity, and inclusion (DEI) in their work to ultimately deepen impact for the kids, families, and communities we serve. You and your organization will be supported on this journey by your peers, the program partners, and an ecosystem of DEI-focused individuals and organizations. You will reflect on the current state of DEI in your organization, map out and implement a plan to achieving your DEI goals, and collectively build the consciousness, resolve, and capacity you need to more deeply integrate equity in your work.



Key benefits of Advance DEI include:
  • Opportunity to assess your organization’s health with DEI assessments at the start, midpoint, and end of the initiative
  • Participation in a cohort-based learning experience including annual live convenings, monthly webinars, access to online resources, and more
  • Access to tailored technical assistance from DEI specialists
  • Opportunities to learn from the experiences of your peer organization and contribute to the collective knowledge and understanding of the field

Should you accept the invitation, your participation in this program is fully funded. However, all participating organizations must commit to:

  • Full participation by a three-leader team for the 2.5 year program duration (Jan 2019 – Apr 2021). The three-leader team must include:
    • A senior leader with positional-authority to approve resource investment, break down organizational barriers, and model leadership commitment, e.g. CEO
    • A leader who will drive the work day-to-day and inherits the plans of the group, e.g. CHRO or Director of Equity, and
    • A third leader of the organization’s choosing (e.g., Board or Staff member)
  • In-person attendance by the three-leader team at both convenings (April 2019, March/April 2020).
  • Willingness and ability to administer an annual DEI baseline survey in Q1 2019, 2020, and 2021.

FAQs

Q: What is [INSERT PROGRAM NAME]? What are its key components?

A: [INSERT PROGRAM NAME] is a joint effort between the Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, Catalyst:Ed, and Promise54 to provide DEI support and resources to a subset of BMGF grantees. The program experience consists of DEI assessments, two in-person convenings (one per year), monthly check-ins, and ongoing and individualized technical assistance.

Q: What will grantees gain from participating in this program?

A: Grantees will strengthen their capacity to more deeply integrate equity into their work. In addition, they will get exclusive access to proprietary DEI resources and tailored supports from DEI specialists.

Q: Is there a cost to participate? What are grantees committing to by participating in this program?

A: All financial costs related to the program experience will be covered by BMGF. By participating in the program, grantees are committing three organizational leaders to participate in a a two and a half year cohort experience that will include two in-person convenings (April 2019 and March/April 2020). They are also committing to administer an annual DEI survey in Q1 2019, 2020, and 2021, create a plan for forward progress based on findings, and leverage program resources to implement that plan. See below for additional details on time commitments.

Q: When does the program start and when will it be completed?

A: The program will run from January 2019—April 2021.
